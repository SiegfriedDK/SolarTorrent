Legal, Disclaimer & How It Works

Last updated: 2026-03-21 — SolarTorrent v2 with community moderation

SolarTorrent is a decentralized, community-moderated protocol. Content validation is performed by the community through on-chain voting — no single entity decides what stays or gets removed.

1 Nature of the Protocol

SolarTorrent is a decentralized application (dApp) running as a smart contract on the DERO blockchain, served via the TELA standard. It functions as an on-chain catalog of magnet links — metadata pointers to content distributed via the BitTorrent protocol.

The protocol does not host, store, transmit, or distribute any files. It stores only metadata: infohash strings, titles, descriptions, category labels, and magnet URIs. The actual content referenced by these magnet links exists solely on the BitTorrent peer-to-peer network, entirely outside the scope of this application.

2 Decentralized Community Moderation

SolarTorrent uses a hybrid moderation system: content is validated or invalidated by the community through on-chain voting, with the contract owner retaining emergency removal powers only.

When a user submits a magnet link, it enters the catalog with a "Pending" status. The community then decides its fate through two actions:

  • Validate — confirms the torrent matches its description and is legitimate content. Each DERO address can cast one vote per torrent.
  • Invalidate — flags the torrent as non-conforming. The voter must select a reason: "Doesn't match the description", "Pornography", or "Ultra violence".

All votes are permanently recorded on-chain with the voter's address (ringsize 2 required). Votes cannot be changed or retracted once cast.

3 Validation & Invalidation Flow

The following diagram illustrates the complete lifecycle of a torrent submission, from deposit to community verdict:

Auto-Approval Conditions

A torrent is automatically approved when it reaches at least 20 validation votes and the validation-to-invalidation ratio exceeds 5:1. Upon approval, the smart contract automatically refunds the 0.1 DERO deposit to the submitter's wallet.

Auto-Removal Conditions

A torrent is automatically removed when it reaches at least 10 invalidation votes and the invalidation-to-validation ratio exceeds 5:1. Upon removal, the smart contract sends the 0.1 DERO deposit to the contract owner as a penalty.

Between these thresholds, torrents remain in "Pending" status and are visible in the catalog, allowing users to evaluate them and cast informed votes.

4 Invalidation Reasons

When casting an invalidation vote, the voter must select one of three predefined reasons, which is permanently stored on-chain:

  • Doesn't match the description — the actual content behind the magnet link does not correspond to the title, description, or category provided by the submitter.
  • Pornography — the content contains pornographic material.
  • Ultra violence — the content contains extreme or gratuitous violence.

The smart contract enforces these as the only valid reasons. Any other value is rejected by the contract. This ensures structured, analyzable moderation data on-chain.

5 Anti-Spam Deposit

Every submission requires a deposit of 0.1 DERO (10,000 atomic units). This deposit serves as an economic deterrent against spam and fraudulent submissions. The deposit outcome depends entirely on community voting:

  • If the community approves the torrent, the deposit is automatically refunded to the submitter.
  • If the community removes the torrent, the deposit is sent to the contract owner.
  • While the torrent remains in "Pending" status, the deposit is held by the smart contract.

The deposit amount can be adjusted by the contract owner to respond to changing network conditions or DERO value.

6 Donations

SolarTorrent includes two donation mechanisms:

  • Donate to uploader — any user can send DERO directly to the person who submitted a torrent. The smart contract reads the donation amount from the transaction and forwards it in full to the submitter's address. No commission is taken.
  • Support SolarTorrent — users can donate to the project maintainers via a dedicated button. This is a direct wallet-to-wallet transfer, independent of the smart contract.

All donations are voluntary, irreversible, and recorded on the DERO blockchain.

7 Magnet Links & Content

A magnet link is a URI scheme that identifies content by its cryptographic hash (infohash) rather than by the location of a specific server. Storing a magnet link on-chain is functionally equivalent to storing a hash string — it is a mathematical identifier, not a copy of the content.

SolarTorrent does not and cannot verify the actual content behind any magnet link. The metadata (title, description, category) is provided by the submitter and may not accurately represent the actual content. This is precisely why community validation exists — to crowdsource content verification.

8 Privacy & Identity

DERO is a privacy-focused blockchain using homomorphic encryption. However, all write operations on SolarTorrent (submitting, voting, donating) require ringsize 2, which means the signer's address is publicly visible for those transactions.

Read operations (browsing, searching, filtering the catalog) do not require any wallet interaction and leave no on-chain trace. The XSWD protocol ensures that private keys never leave the user's local device.

9 Immutability & Blockchain Permanence

All data written to the DERO blockchain is permanent by design. This includes torrent metadata, submitter addresses, all validation and invalidation votes with reasons, donation transactions, and status changes. The "RemoveTorrent" function changes the status flag but does not erase the underlying data. Historical states of the smart contract remain accessible to anyone running a DERO node.

10 Owner Emergency Powers

The contract owner (the wallet that deployed the smart contract) retains limited emergency powers:

  • RemoveTorrent — immediately sets a torrent's status to "removed" (status 3), bypassing community voting. This is intended for extreme cases such as illegal content that requires immediate action before the community threshold is reached.
  • SetDeposit — adjusts the anti-spam deposit amount.
  • Withdraw — withdraws accumulated penalty fees from the smart contract.
  • TransferOwnership — transfers the owner role to another DERO address.
  • UpdateCode — updates the smart contract code (for bug fixes and feature additions).

The owner cannot modify or delete votes, alter torrent metadata, or interfere with the community moderation thresholds. These are hardcoded in the smart contract logic.

SolarTorrent is provided "as is" without warranty of any kind. The developers make no representations regarding the accuracy of catalog entries, the availability of the DERO network, or the security of the smart contract code. Use is entirely at your own risk.

12 Open Source & Auditability

The smart contract code is publicly auditable on the DERO blockchain by anyone running a node. The frontend application is stored immutably in TELA-DOC-1 contracts. All logic governing validation, invalidation, deposits, and donations is transparently verifiable on-chain. Code is law.

13 Governing Law

SolarTorrent is a decentralized protocol with no geographic jurisdiction. Users are solely responsible for ensuring their use complies with the laws of their own jurisdiction. If the use of decentralized torrent catalogs is prohibited where you live, do not use this application.
