Legal & Disclaimer

Last updated: 2026-03-19 — Effective upon deployment to DERO mainnet

SolarTorrent is a decentralized, permissionless protocol. No entity controls, operates, or moderates the content indexed on-chain. Use at your own discretion and in compliance with your local jurisdiction.

1 Nature of the Protocol

SolarTorrent is a decentralized application (dApp) running as a set of smart contracts on the DERO blockchain, served via the TELA standard. It functions as an on-chain catalog of magnet links — metadata pointers to content distributed via the BitTorrent protocol.

The protocol does not host, store, transmit, or distribute any files. It stores only metadata: infohash strings, titles, descriptions, category labels, license identifiers, and magnet URIs. The actual content referenced by these magnet links exists solely on the BitTorrent peer-to-peer network, entirely outside the scope of this application.

2 No Central Operator

Once deployed on the DERO blockchain, the smart contract executes autonomously according to its code. There is no central server, no company, no legal entity, and no individual operating SolarTorrent as a service. The contract owner role exists solely for anti-spam moderation of catalog entries and has no ability to alter, censor, or control the DERO blockchain itself.

The TELA standard ensures that the application code is stored immutably in TELA-DOC-1 contracts and served locally by the user's own Engram wallet — no third-party server is involved in delivering the application to the user.

3 User Responsibilities

By interacting with SolarTorrent, you acknowledge and accept the following responsibilities:

  • You will only submit magnet links pointing to content that you have the legal right to distribute, or that is in the public domain, or that is licensed under an open license (Creative Commons, GPL, MIT, Apache, etc.).
  • You will not submit links to content that infringes copyright, contains illegal material, or violates the laws of your jurisdiction.
  • You understand that submitting a torrent entry requires a DERO transaction with ringsize 2, meaning your wallet address is publicly associated with the submission.
  • You accept that the anti-spam deposit (currently 0.1 DERO) is non-refundable if your submission is rejected by the catalog moderator.
  • You acknowledge that all data written to the DERO blockchain is permanent and cannot be deleted by any party, including the contract owner.

4 Magnet Links & Content

A magnet link is a URI scheme that identifies content by its cryptographic hash (infohash) rather than by the location of a specific server. Storing a magnet link on-chain is functionally equivalent to storing a hash string — it is a mathematical identifier, not a copy of the content.

SolarTorrent does not and cannot verify the actual content behind any magnet link. The metadata (title, description, license) is provided by the submitter and may not accurately represent the actual content. Users should exercise their own judgment and verify content independently before downloading.

5 Anti-Spam Mechanism

To deter spam and low-quality submissions, a deposit of DERO is required when submitting a new catalog entry. This deposit is handled entirely by the smart contract:

  • If the entry is approved by the moderator, the deposit is automatically refunded to the submitter's wallet via the smart contract.
  • If the entry is rejected, the deposit is retained by the smart contract as a fee. The moderator may withdraw accumulated fees.
  • The deposit amount can be adjusted by the contract owner to adapt to changing conditions.

6 Immutability & Blockchain Permanence

All data written to the DERO blockchain is permanent by design. This includes:

  • Torrent metadata (hash, title, description, magnet link)
  • Submitter wallet addresses
  • Approval/rejection status
  • Transaction history

The "RemoveTorrent" function changes the status flag of an entry to "removed" but does not delete the underlying data from the blockchain. Historical states of the smart contract remain accessible to anyone running a DERO node.

7 Privacy Considerations

DERO is a privacy-focused blockchain using homomorphic encryption. However, interactions with SolarTorrent require ringsize 2 for write operations (submit, approve, reject), which means the signer's address is publicly visible for those transactions. Read operations (browsing the catalog) do not require any wallet interaction and leave no on-chain trace.

The XSWD protocol ensures that your wallet's private keys never leave your local device. SolarTorrent only communicates with your wallet through permissioned WebSocket calls that you explicitly approve in Engram.
